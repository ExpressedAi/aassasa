Raycast AI: # Triangulation Explained

Hey Money, triangulation is a powerful technique for determining location. Let me break this down for you:

## Basic Triangulation Concept

Triangulation uses distance or angle measurements from known reference points to determine an unknown position. The name comes from the triangle formed in the process, but the specifics depend on the type of triangulation.

## Types of Triangulation

### Distance-Based Triangulation (Trilateration)

This uses **distances** from known points:

- With **2 reference points**: You get a circle of possible locations (intersection of two circles)
- With **3 reference points**: You get a precise location (where three circles intersect)

### Angle-Based Triangulation

This uses **angles** from known points:

- With **2 reference points**: You can determine position if you know the angles from both points
- With **3 reference points**: Provides redundancy and greater accuracy

## Real-World Examples

| Method | Application | Minimum Points | Optimal Points |
|:-------|:------------|:---------------|:--------------|
| GPS | Global positioning | 3 satellites | 4+ satellites |
| Cell tower | Phone location | 2 towers | 3+ towers |
| Radio direction | Finding transmitters | 2 stations | 3+ stations |
| Land surveying | Mapping | 2 known points | 3+ known points |

## Mathematical Basis

For trilateration with three points, the position is where three spheres intersect:

\[(x - x_1)^2 + (y - y_1)^2 + (z - z_1)^2 = r_1^2\]
\[(x - x_2)^2 + (y - y_2)^2 + (z - z_2)^2 = r_2^2\]
\[(x - x_3)^2 + (y - y_3)^2 + (z - z_3)^2 = r_3^2\]

Where \((x_i, y_i, z_i)\) are known points and \(r_i\) are measured distances.

## Bottom Line

You technically need a minimum of 2 reference points for basic triangulation, but 3 points gives you:
1. A unique solution in most cases
2. Error checking capability
3. Higher accuracy

The more reference points you have, the more accurate your position determination becomes.
